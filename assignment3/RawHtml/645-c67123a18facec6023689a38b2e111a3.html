Leicester City 5-3 Manchester United: Jamie Vardy Inspires Fantastic Foxes Comeback

By Aiden Cusick
on 21 Sep 2014

Leicester City striker Jamie Vardy inspired the Foxes to an incredible comeback as they beat Manchester United 5-3 at the King Power Stadium.


The former non-league striker had a hand in all five goals and scored one for himself on his first start of the season.


First half strikes from Robin van Persie and Angel Di Maria had put the visitors two up inside a quarter of an hour, but a header from Leo Ulloa immediately dragged the Foxes level.


Ander Herrera extended the advantage but a David Nugent penalty got Leicester back in the game before goals from Esteban Cambiasso and Vardy pulled off a remarkable turnaround.


Youngster Tyler Blackett later saw red for United after hauling down the former Fleetwood man, conceding the penalty from which Ulloa added Leicester's fifth.


Two of the Premier League's mid-table teams went head-to-head in England's East Midlands but much of the game's attacking play would not have looked out of place on Europe's most prestigious stage. The defending, however, was perhaps more at home in the Evo-Stik Northern Premier.


Both sides held identical records going into the game: Played four, won one, drawn two, lost another.


But while Nigel Pearson will be delighted with Leicester's start, having faced four of last season's top 10, United counterpart Louis van Gaal will have been hoping for more with Swansea and Sunderland their only opponents from last season's top flight.


Their trip to the King Power was their third in a row against a newly promoted side and would be the toughest so far, on paper.


Leicester's young squad, which has been carefully assembled over the best part of three years, ran away with the Championship last season thanks to a home record which boasted just two league defeats.


But this was a new Manchester United, or so we thought, boosted by the world-class additions of Falcao, Di Maria and Daley Blind, and buoyed by a 4-0 hammering of QPR in their last outing.


It took 10 minutes for van Gaal's men to deliver the game's first chance. Di Maria surged through the middle and, in his stride, slipped in van Persie, whose effort was blocked by the legs of an alert Kasper Schmeichel.


It was an early warning for the Foxes, who were behind three minutes later. Falcao's turn escaped Ritchie De Laet down the left and he delivered an inch perfect cross to the far post where the Dutchman was lurking to stoop home, via a deflection.


And the visitors wasted no time in doubling their advantage when Wayne Rooney strode forward, unchallenged, and found Di Maria on the edge of the box. The British record transfer did the rest: gracefully lifting the ball over the stranded Schmeichel without a split second of hesitation.


The ex-Real Madrid man was again making a mockery of his former side's willingness to usher him out of Santiago Bernabeu.


But for all their attacking exuberance, question marks remain over United's new-look back line, which was carved open straight from the kick-off as the home side levelled through their own record purchase, the Â£8m Ulloa.


Vardy, returning from a thigh injury, skipped round Marcos Rojo and swept an inch perfect cross towards Ulloa, who rose above Rafael and powered his header inside the near post.


It was a priceless goal for Leicester, halting United's new-found momentum and temporarily bringing last season's seventh-placed side back down to earth.


But United were back in the swing of things shortly after the restart. Within four minutes, Falcao was millimetres away from bettering Di Maria's earlier goal: Rooney's dinked pass sat up well for the Colombian, whose volleyed lob off evaded Schmeichel and came crashing back off the crossbar.


And they were back in control within 10 minutes - thanks to another new signing getting in on the act. Di Maria unleashed a low strike from 25 yards, which appeared to be going wide until Herrera stuck out a heel to change the direction of the ball and send Schmeichel scrambling the wrong way.


They say you're at your most vulnerable after scoring a goal and United seemed determined to prove it, this time conceding four minutes after Herrera had restored their two-goal cushion.


It was the troublesome Vardy at the thick of things again. Rafael was bullied - and probably fouled - by the former Fleetwood man, who surged into the box and awaited the Brazilian's retaliation. It came, referee Mark Clattenburg pointed to the spot and Nugent - last season's top scorer with 20 - assumed responsibility to fire straight down the middle of David De Gea's goal.


Leicester sensed blood and it was only two minutes before they were level. Dean Hammond's shot hit Vardy and fell into the path of Cambiasso, who gratefully guided the ball into De Gea's bottom corner.


It would only get better from there and when United lost possession in the Foxes half, former Old Trafford youngster De Laet freed Vardy, who raced clear to sidefoot past De Gea with 11 minutes remaining.


The King Power was rocking to the tune of "we want five" from the home faithful and when Vardy was again played in over the top, Blackett decided to grant them their wish: hauling down the man who was plucked from Conference football just two years ago, inside the box.


Ulloa was on duty this time, picking out the bottom right and taking his league goals tally to five in five matches. Thankfully, for the deflated former champions, that would be that.


Teams:


Leicester: Schmeichel; De Laet, Morgan, Moore, Konchesky; Hammond, Cambiasso (King 71), Drinkwater; Vardy (Schlupp 85), Nugent (James 75), Ulloa.


United: De Gea; Rafael, Evans (Smalling 30), Blackett, Rojo; Blind, Herrera, Di Maria (Mata 76), Rooney; Falcao (Januzaj 72), van Persie.
