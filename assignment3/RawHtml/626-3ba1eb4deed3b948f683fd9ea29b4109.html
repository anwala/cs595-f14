Ooh naa naa, that's my name!

From capes to handbags, branding your initials on your favourite item has been appearing everywhere recently. One of the first people to do this was everyones favourite sex writer and continuous question asker Carrie Bradshaw, who's whole life was turned upside down when she lost her name necklace. 

 But it's not just sitcoms that this trend has been making an appearance in. At this years Vogue Fashion Night Out, personalisation was a highlight; brands were offering visitors the luxury of embellishing their purchases with their names. For those with a bigger budget, they could follow in the footsteps of Poppy Delevingne by purchasing a Superdry Leather Jacket which they could then customise. For those who didn't want to buy a leather jacket, they could pop to H&M and slap their initials on a canvas shopper. 

Poppy isn't the only celeb joining in on the trend of scrawling their name across their clothing. At a recent performance, Lily Allen was seen wearing this heavily beaded personalised top by Ashish.


One of the trends we can't escape this season (and would we want to?), is the blanket cape. The British clothing house Burberry is no doubt dominating this trend. Every big name in the celeb and fashion world has been seen with a beautiful cape draped over their shoulders. But it's not just the cape trend Burberry are engaging in, they are also putting the initials on their capes! Burberry, you can do no wrong.


I love luggage. Like literally love it so much. My dream is to one day earn enough money so that I can buy a Louis Vuitton suitcase. Luggage has just always had the association of glamour and luxury. How could you make something which is so luxurious become even more alluring? Add your initials to it! Just imagine how Hollywood you'll feel with your magazine in one hand, Pret stuffed in your hand luggage, sunnies pushing your hair back and most importantly beautiful personalised luggage carefully rolling behind you (even if it is just about to be squeezed in the Easyjet overhead compartments). 


Don't feel down, it's not just bigger budgets that can afford personalised luggage. I found quite a few cheaper companies that stocked monogram travel gear too, as shown below.

This travel set is made by Annabel Chaffer and is certainly a lot nicer on the bank balance compared to a LV purchase.


If you're not much of a luggage lover but still want some sort of bag, theres plenty of choices of monogram clutches. I found that the best ones were actually on Etsy.



Here are just two of the bags found on Etsy.

What I love the most about this trend is that it is the epitmation of putting your mark on it. Slight problem is that my initials don't look that great (OL just doesn't have a good ring to it)  but for all you with fab initials, hope you enjoy the trend!

How I'm dealing with my Fashion Week hangover

Fashion Week, great for the soul, bad for the body. Long hours mixed with a lot of fun results in a gut full of sweets, Itsu and champagne. At the time this was of course very enjoyable but I'm certainly feeling the effects. I am however, one of the lucky ones that's managed to have a couple of days off recover. And here's what I've been doing: 

 Lack of sleep

 Turned the alarm off. As complicated as that.
Horrendous skin

 Seriously, my skin is horrendous. Spots, spots and more spots resting on a bed of dullness. This was no doubt a result of days and days of make-up which was topped up throughout the day. As well as the make-up was the London pollution and the crap food. To help clear my blemishes I've used this face mask bought from my local natural health shop Infinity Foods.


 Bad diet

 Managing a healthy diet is particularly important for me, not just for vanity, but more importantly for my health. I suffer from a lot of intolerances which means I can be victim to constantly feeling ill. Eating on the go for the past week, has meant that I had to consume a lot of meals that could have potentially contained the foods I usually avoid. In addition, there were times where I was certain I couldn't eat anything that was available, so I had to resort to sweets. There were also the times where I just ate sweets because well, they looked really nice and they were free. 

 To help me get over my poor diet, I've been cutting down on artificial products (something I try to do anyway), cut down on processed carbs and really make sure I'm sticking to my gluten free, egg free and dairy free diet (yes it's a nightmare but it can be done). My meals have included lots of veg and lots of white meat (I'm cutting out red meat but that's for environmental reasons). Although I'd like to portray myself as one of those uber disciplined 'instafit' type, I do have a sweet tooth. To fulfil this, I made gluten, egg and dairy free cookies which are almost sugar free! 

This is my jerk bowl. It's so easy and actually so yummy. I grilled jerk and cumin seasoned turkey with onions and courgette which I then mixed with boiled green beans, broccoli and cauliflower. Once they were all together, I added tomato puree and seasoned with more jerk, cumin and pepper. The white you can see is fake cheese which is a close copy to actual cheese. I grill everything with coconut oil. It's a lot lighter and a lot better for you compared to some other oils. 



These are my egg, dairy, gluten free cookies. When I bake I do everything by eye so I can't really put the recipe up. I'll do them again and be more careful with the ingredients.


That feeling of pollution all over your body

You know that feeling where you feel so grubby you just want to go to a spa and get a full body scrub? Well I had that but couldn't afford a spa. I did however, have a Frank coffee scrub in my Shopcade goodie bag which I smothered my body with.



After doing all of the above, I'm starting to feel almost back to my original self.
