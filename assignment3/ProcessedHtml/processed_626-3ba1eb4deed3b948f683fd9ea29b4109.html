From capes to handbags, branding your initials on your favourite item has been appearing everywhere recently. One of the first people to do this was everyones favourite sex writer and continuous question asker Carrie Bradshaw, who's whole life was turned upside down when she lost her name necklace. But it's not just sitcoms that this trend has been making an appearance in. At this years Vogue Fashion Night Out, personalisation was a highlight; brands were offering visitors the luxury of embellishing their purchases with their names. For those with a bigger budget, they could follow in the footsteps of Poppy Delevingne by purchasing a Superdry Leather Jacket which they could then customise. For those who didn't want to buy a leather jacket, they could pop to H&M and slap their initials on a canvas shopper. [46][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-25%2Bat%2B19.09.47.png] Poppy isn't the only celeb joining in on the trend of scrawling their name across their clothing. At a recent performance, Lily Allen was seen wearing this heavily beaded personalised top by Ashish. [47][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-24%2Bat%2B18.09.22.png] One of the trends we can't escape this season (and would we want to?), is the blanket cape. The British clothing house Burberry is no doubt dominating this trend. Every big name in the celeb and fashion world has been seen with a beautiful cape draped over their shoulders. But it's not just the cape trend Burberry are engaging in, they are also putting the initials on their capes! Burberry, you can do no wrong. [48][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-25%2Bat%2B19.20.23.png] I love luggage. Like literally love it so much. My dream is to one day earn enough money so that I can buy a Louis Vuitton suitcase. Luggage has just always had the association of glamour and luxury. How could you make something which is so luxurious become even more alluring? Add your initials to it! Just imagine how Hollywood you'll feel with your magazine in one hand, Pret stuffed in your hand luggage, sunnies pushing your hair back and most importantly beautiful personalised luggage carefully rolling behind you (even if it is just about to be squeezed in the Easyjet overhead compartments). [49][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-25%2Bat%2B19.07.34.png] Don't feel down, it's not just bigger budgets that can afford personalised luggage. [50][luggage.png] This travel set is made by Annabel Chaffer and is certainly a lot nicer on the bank balance compared to a LV purchase. [51][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-25%2Bat%2B21.20.41.png] [52][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-25%2Bat%2B21.23.02.png] Here are just two of the bags found on Etsy. Long hours mixed with a lot of fun results in a gut full of sweets, Itsu and champagne. At the time this was of course very enjoyable but I'm certainly feeling the effects. I am however, one of the lucky ones that's managed to have a couple of days off recover. And here's what I've been doing: Lack of sleep Turned the alarm off. As complicated as that. Horrendous skin Seriously, my skin is horrendous. Spots, spots and more spots resting on a bed of dullness. This was no doubt a result of days and days of make-up which was topped up throughout the day. As well as the make-up was the London pollution and the crap food. To help clear my blemishes I've used this face mask bought from my local natural health shop Infinity Foods. [81][facemask.jpeg] Bad diet Managing a healthy diet is particularly important for me, not just for vanity, but more importantly for my health. I suffer from a lot of intolerances which means I can be victim to constantly feeling ill. Eating on the go for the past week, has meant that I had to consume a lot of meals that could have potentially contained the foods I usually avoid. In addition, there were times where I was certain I couldn't eat anything that was available, so I had to resort to sweets. There were also the times where I just ate sweets because well, they looked really nice and they were free. To help me get over my poor diet, I've been cutting down on artificial products (something I try to do anyway), cut down on processed carbs and really make sure I'm sticking to my gluten free, egg free and dairy free diet (yes it's a nightmare but it can be done). My meals have included lots of veg and lots of white meat (I'm cutting out red meat but that's for environmental reasons). Although I'd like to portray myself as one of those uber disciplined 'instafit' type, I do have a sweet tooth. To fulfil this, I made gluten, egg and dairy free cookies which are almost sugar free! [82][dinner.jpeg] This is my jerk bowl. It's so easy and actually so yummy. I grilled jerk and cumin seasoned turkey with onions and courgette which I then mixed with boiled green beans, broccoli and cauliflower. Once they were all together, I added tomato puree and seasoned with more jerk, cumin and pepper. The white you can see is fake cheese which is a close copy to actual cheese. I grill everything with coconut oil. It's a lot lighter and a lot better for you compared to some other oils. [83][Screen%2BShot%2B2014-09-23%2Bat%2B22.08.48.png] These are my egg, dairy, gluten free cookies. When I bake I do everything by eye so I can't really put the recipe up. I'll do them again and be more careful with the ingredients. That feeling of pollution all over your body You know that feeling where you feel so grubby you just want to go to a spa and get a full body scrub? Well I had that but couldn't afford a spa. I did however, have a Frank coffee scrub in my Shopcade goodie bag which I smothered my body with. I have time to pick an outfit and don't have to do my make-up on the train. When I get back to the Shopcade Pop-up, I remember how much I'm enjoying this week. The pop-up closes and its time to start getting the area ready for the V.I.P party. We quickly rush over to Pret and I eat the same meal that I've eaten every day (I'm dairy, gluten and egg intolerant so very limited to what I can eat). Glitter Lips still on, we are ready to start the party. Myself and my friend Lucy are manning the door which is surprisingly a really fun task. You get to speak to loads of different people which is not only interesting, but very useful for networking. When it comes to doing tasks like this, there's a tip I'd give to everyone: research your guests! As I said in an earlier post, I don't really watch television so I'm not up to date with the reality stars. Rather than find the time to watch tele, I googled the guests. Please interns, you do not want to embarrass yourselves, do your research. To be clear, I don't mean this exclusively to 'famous' people, know your industry people and those that are good friends with the company you are representing; really get to know your guest list! This being said, I want to be clear that you should treat all guests with respect. There's nothing impressive about being a 'door bitch'. Everyone that is standing waiting to get in, wants to come to your event and that's a compliment. In fact, they've chosen to come to your event when they are probably exhausted from a long day on their feet. So, when it comes to hosting a guest list, remember to really get to grips with who's going to be coming and never forget to BE NICE! The nights over and I'm excitedly looking through my goodie bag during my taxi ride to my friends house. It's lovely that I get to stay with a friend from Uni, especially after this somewhat surreal week. We giggle over her horrendous graduation picture (sorry Em) and we talk as if we are back in our disgusting student house. I've got one day off then its back working on a LFW closing party. It's the last day of LFW and I'm collecting make-up in Covent Garden whilst balancing the social media coverage for tonight's party. This evening's event is for a different company, so I need to quickly pick up from where I last left off. I'm slightly panicking as I sit having my lunch opposite Fashion Scout: 'Where can I find clothes for the event tonight?'. I'm looking through my contacts in hope to not disappoint my boss. I notice two people I met at the Bora Aksu show and I smile although I don't think they recognise me. I carry on wishfully scrolling through my contacts until I notice a boy sitting at my table. 'Hi, I met you the other day' Kieran says. I'm so flattered he remembers me! He asks me what I'm doing and I explain. Within seconds Kieran turns from industry friend to genuine angel sent from heaven. He gives me the press office number of a fantastic company and I feel a massive weight off my shoulders. I want this to be a little lesson to not only myself, but to other interns; always do your networking (stay tuned for my post on this). If I hadn't spoken to Kieran earlier on in the week, he wouldn't have saved my bum when I needed it most! Contacts saved onto my phone, I've finally completed all my pre-party errands so I hop over to the venue in Shoreditch. Absolutely exhausted from the week, all I want to do is get in my bed. I make sure I've done everything I need to do before I call a taxi. I'm finally home and I throw my outfit onto the pile of everything else I've worn this week. I think to myself how I'll clean it tomorrow and literally fall into bed. I arrive to the Shopcade pop-up and I get excited again. My boss greets me and explains cheerily that there's a different task I have to do (I feel very thankful that I've got to do these 'alternative' tasks twice now). I get briefed by the two lovely boys of Candid Magazine who want me to go down to Somerset House and report back on 'street style'. Before I head down, I remember how bold everyone looks on the cobbles so I quickly visit the the girls at the Glitter Lips stand and glam up. Approaching smartly dressed boys at the BFC Courtyard is surprisingly easy; practically everyone wants their photo taken. Note book in hand, I take down designer after designer, name after name. I remember that I've got a press pass, so decide to use the 'special' toilets (anyone that knows me will know the excitement I would feel towards this). In the buyers and press lounge everyone is sitting on laptops chatting away. I feel proud to be a part of it all. [162][10632854_10154535043905386_6366591521938619823_n.jpg] Back at the Shopcade Pop-up there's such a buzz. Everyone is having a really good time. I grab another intern and we head over to Fashion Scout to spread the word on what's going on. A girl comes over and asks to take a picture of me for a blog she works for. I AM ABSOLUTELY [DEL: DELIGHTED :DEL] ECSTATIC. As I said earlier, practically everyone at Fashion Week is thrilled to have their photo taken, in fact it's almost the prime goal. [163][10646856_893556257338853_6022939454537732544_n.jpg] I'm back at the Pop-up and I've had my make-up done ready for tonight. Myself and another intern quickly grab some dinner then it's time for the We are Collision party. The room starts filling up and I see a lot of familiar faces (by familiar, I mean people I've seen at various shows and events this week). I get talking to a boy who recognises me from the Bora Aksu show. He explains to me how he has to miss the end of LFW because Uni begins again tomorrow. That's the thing about LFW, everyone is grafting their 'bums off'. Everyone knows that to make it, you'll probably be doing 20 hour days for free. But this is something everyone does, and everyone does quite happily. After a couple of hours and some ushering of a few famous faces to have their picture taken, I'm ready to go home. I get on the packed last train and eat all my free dried fruit and drink my coconut water. The boys next to me seem very confused by my glitter lips (which can I say, stayed on all day after having two meals and hot drinks!) which I completely forgot I was wearing. The two hour journey is spent with all of us strangers discussing everything from asian weddings to healthy ice cream. I get into bed and set my alarm. Bring on tomorrow!
