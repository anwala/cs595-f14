This month’s Maven collection is all about embracing the dark, mystic beauty of the Halloween season, and we’ve got this fashion-week-meets-fright-night nail art look to help you do just that. [23]Dana (It Girl) is the perfect addition to all your spooky nail art because it contains a fluorescent brightener that will illuminate under the black light at your big Halloween party and give your nails a electric blue sheen even in the light of day. There are so many fun and festive nail looks you can create using Dana: ghosts, claws, cat’s eyes, fangs, you name it. For this look, we’re putting a fresh coat on a timeless classic: ghoul-in-the-dark skulls. Materials: The [24]Plié Wand™ [25]Plié Wand Creativity Kit (striping brush and dotting tool) [26]Oxygen Smoothing Base Coat [27]Oxygen Performance Top Coat [28]Dana (It Girl) [29]Jet (It Girl) Instructions: 1. Apply a base coat and allow to dry. Then polish all nails in [30]Dana (It Girl). 2. Pour a small amount of [31]Jet (It Girl) onto a piece of foil. Attach the striping brush to the [32]Plié Wand and dip into [33]Jet. Draw a small, curved wedge shape on either side of the nail. This will be the jawline of each skull. 3. Still using the striping brush, draw three or four short, thin vertical lines on the tip of each nail. These will be the teeth. 4. Detach the striping brush, attach the dotting tool to the [34]Plié Wand, and dip back into [35]Jet. Draw two small dots in the center of the nail and carefully connect them in a point, making an upside down heart. This will be each skull’s nose. 5. For the eyes, draw two slightly larger dots in the upper third of each nail. 6. Allow plenty of time for your work to dry, and then finish with a top coat. Now go show off your glowing skull nails under the black light! Or any other light. Your black magic mani will look enchanting either way. But first, of course, you’ve got to add double-trouble [36]Dana to your Maven box. [37]Right this way, my sweet.
